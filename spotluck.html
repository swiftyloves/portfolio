What's the Challenge?

The U-M Taiwanese students who just move into Ann Arbor need to cook by themselves. However, they have limited cook knowledge and cooking skills. The students are only capable to cook a small range of cuisine and they cook the same food repeatedly.

I conducted user interview to collect what students’ opinions about creating new cuisines and their pain points on cooking.

I don’t have time thinking of cooking new cuisines, especially on weekdays.

I always but the same ingredients since at least that makes me feel safe.
I'm very busy. The last time I try to cook new cuisines is winter break.

Competitive Analysis

When searching for how to cook, most students buy ingredients and search the recipes to see what ingredients they have could make. Therefore I choose three online recipes. I also survey one new service which provides ingredients to save the grocery shopping.

Service Frustraction
Cooking.nytimes.com

It is not targeting at students thus some of the recipes might be too time-consuming and difficult for students to make.

iCook.tw

Users need to look up the English name of ingredients in order to buy the ingredients in supermarket.

StudentRecipes.com

The information organization is messy because the steps or methods are uploaded by students and there is no strict rules about it.

BlueApron.com

It’s more expensive than go grocery shopping.

User Feedback

I interviewed 9 stakeholders about their cooking experience and asked feedback for the 16 ideas. The key findings are:

  • Most students care more about time, they don't like to involve others since it's time-consuming.
  • Students who think of cuisine before the ingredients have higher involved in cooking environments when they were in Taiwan.
  • Most students evaluate themselves in limited or middle cooking skills.
  • Most students take bus to grocery shopping and they want to reduce the amount of going to supermarket.

The top-three ideas most users favor and their thoughts of them:

1. Weekly Challenge

Recommend users new ingredients they have not tried based on the supermarket sales. Make it like a game, a "challenge," for users. Provide where to get the ingredients and the related recipes.

  • Care about money - combine grocery sale could save money
  • Like to have fun - game makes it looks fun
  • Be Inclined to think of ingredient or cuisines first - some like ideas about ingredients, some prefer recommend cuisine

2. Real-Time Recommendation

Real-time recommend users new cuisines when they are in grocery shopping based on what they put in shopping carts and what they have at home.

  • Care about time - let users know what else needed to cook new cuisine, then they could buy together to avoid additional grocery shopping
  • Visualization cuisine options - users will see what they could cook based on the ingredients collection they’re creating
  • Think about further details - this also make users to think about the details like how to calculate what they have in refrigerators

3. Food Puzzle

Use puzzle to brainstorm new and better combination of ingredients to cook new cuisines.

  • Inspiration - It provide users inspiration on what to eat.
  • Ingredient-first - People who think of ingredients before cuisines like it. People who think of the opposite way say they would not use it.
  • Limited cooking skills - This is for users who have limited cooking skills. People who have higher cooking skills say they already know the better combination of ingredients

Personas

Based on the user interview I considered following behavioral variables to create the persona:

  • Cooking Skill
  • Care about Price
  • Healthy
  • Busy
  • Desire for Various Cuisines
  • Make Shopping List
  • Cuisine first — Ingredient first

I created 4 personas including 1 primary, 2 secondary, and 1 anti personas.
