What's the Challenge?

The U-M Taiwanese students who just move into Ann Arbor need to cook by themselves. However, they have limited cook knowledge and cooking skills. The students are only capable to cook a small range of cuisine and they cook the same food repeatedly.

They have following constraints:

Kitchen Newbies

Have limited cook knowledge and cooking skills

New Cuisines

Be tired of same food and want to try something new

Budget

Care about money

Health

Desire to eat healthy

Busy Schedule

Have limit time on cooking

Competitive Analysis

The reason why I choose the first three reasons is when students do not how to cook, they’re inclined to search online and learn from the recipes. These online recipes have their features. The first is a common recipe website, which would be the top-three search results with keyword “Recipe.” The second targets at students and the third is particularly customized for Taiwan people. The forth is opposite to previous three. Instead of finding recipe and buying ingredients, users could just get things they need.

Brainstorm, Sketch, and Storyboard

I brainstormed and sketches the 16 ideas and creaed the 8 scenarios.

User Feedback

I interviewed 9 stakeholders about their cooking experience and asked feedback for the 16 ideas. The key findings are:

  • Most students care more about time and money.
  • Students who think of cuisine before the ingredients have higher involved in cooking environments when they were in Taiwan.
  • Major students evaluate themselves in limited or middle cooking skills.
  • Most students take bus to grocery shopping and they want to reduce the amount of going supermarket.

Personas

Based on the user interview I considered following behavioral variables to create the persona:

  • Cooking Skill
  • Care about Price
  • Healthy
  • Busy
  • Desire for Various Cuisines
  • Make Shopping List
  • Cuisine first — Ingredient first

I created 4 personas including 1 primary, 2 secondary, and 1 anti personas.
